methodology - locating system maps
==================================

Visit the official website of the metro system.  Find the most
prominent system map available.  If no map can be found, search google
images for "<city> metro map" and take the top result.

system map exceptions
---------------------

The following exceptions were noted while searching for official system
maps of the selected metro systems:

- No official map was found for Chongqing or Monterrey.  The specified
  fallback procedure was used to obtain unofficial maps.
- The only map found for Guangzhou was in a screenshot of their mobile
  app.  While not an official map, this was on the official website.
- The website for Metro Madrid offered numerous types of maps, which
  include a "Touristic Map" and a map "with cartographic basis".  The
  map selected was the "Schematic Map", as that is the one that appears
  in the thumbnail link to the maps page from the homepage.
- The Toronto map was apparently recently redesigned to follow a strict
  cartographic plot.  Again, the diagrammatic map was chosen, since
  it is still the one indicated in the thumbnail link to the maps page.

methodology - scoring map styles
================================

Maps were categorized into three buckets:

- Diagrammatic: all lines are situated at 45 or 90 degree angles, roads
  do not appear, geographic features are simplified or non-existent.
- Cartographic/stylized: lines are free-form, major roads appear,
  geographic features are frequent and more-or-less accurate.
- Hybrid: somewhere in between the above two.

These criteria are relatively objective, but there is admittedly some
amount of judgement made.

discussion
==========

Clearly, New York City is in the significant minority of cities whose
official subway map uses a cartographic style.  This is true when
looking at other major North American transit systems, when comparing
to major systems around the world, and even more starkly true when
putting New York City in the context of the world's largest and busiest
metro systems.
